### WARNING: Public text-integrity rebaseline surface can mask post-boot drift

- Laws implicated: Law 10 Immutable OS Image, Law 8 Book of Truth Immediacy, Law 9 Crash on Log Failure.
- Evidence: `Kernel/BookOfTruth.HC` adds `public U0 BookTruthTextBootBaseline(...)`, assigns `bot_text_integrity_baseline_hash=hash_now`, and `Kernel/KExts.HC` exports it for general use.
- Risk: the helper is needed during boot, but after export it appears callable after initialization as a local CLI/API surface. A post-boot caller could replace the expected text hash with the current hash and then let `BookTruthTextIntegrityTick` pass against the new baseline. That does not directly modify the OS image or delete the Book of Truth, but it weakens the integrity evidence expected by WS13-11.
- Suggested remediation: constrain this helper to an early boot/run-level guard or split it into a private boot-only baseline routine plus a read-only status command. If intentional local rebaseline is required, it should emit a distinct tamper-class event and fail-stop or require a sealed reinstall state transition.
