## Findings

### CRITICAL: Law 8 - PIC IRQ record path adds software layers before the UART record

Evidence:

- `Kernel/KInts.HC:5-20` adds `INT_IRQ`, but the handler records via `CALL &BookTruthIRQHook` before EOI rather than composing the record and issuing the serial `out 0x3F8` bytes inline.
- `Kernel/KInts.HC:285-287` implements `BookTruthIRQHook` as a HolyC wrapper around `BookTruthIRQRecord`.
- `Kernel/BookOfTruth.HC:15429-15444` implements `BookTruthIRQRecord`, increments counters, then calls `BookTruthAppend(BOT_EVENT_IRQ,BOT_SOURCE_IRQ,irq)`.

Law basis:

- Law 8 requires interrupt logging inside the IDT handler and as close to hardware as possible.
- Law 8 also forbids a software layer between the hardware event and the `out 0x3F8` serial record, and its required pattern says the log entry is composed and emitted in the same instruction sequence.

Impact:

- The new PIC IRQ coverage is synchronous and before EOI, which is good, but it is not zero-layer hardware-proximate logging.
- The `BookTruthIRQHook -> BookTruthIRQRecord -> BookTruthAppend` chain can be bypassed, patched, or made semantically divergent independently of the handler. That violates the Book of Truth immediacy/hardware-proximity doctrine for the new IRQ vectors.

Required correction:

- Replace the new PIC IRQ path with an inline/minimal logging sequence that composes the IRQ event and emits to COM1 from the handler instruction path before returning from the interrupt.
- Keep the generic HolyC append path only after the serial record has been synchronously emitted, or document a Law 8 amendment if the project intentionally accepts a helper call boundary.
