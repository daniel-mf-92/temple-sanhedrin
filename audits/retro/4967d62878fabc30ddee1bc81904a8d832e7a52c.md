## Findings

### CRITICAL: Reclaim clears sealed Book of Truth entries without a per-entry serial-confirmation proof

- Laws implicated: Law 3 Book of Truth Immutability, Law 9 Resource Supremacy / Crash on Log Failure.
- Evidence: `BookTruthReclaimPage()` selects the oldest sealed slot and executes `MemSet(bot_entries+idx,0,sizeof(CBookOfTruthEntry));`, then clears `bot_sealed_slots[idx]` and decrements `bot_count` / `bot_sealed_count` in `Kernel/BookOfTruth.HC` lines 2109-2124.
- Evidence: the guard only requires `bot_seal_mode`, `bot_sealed_slots`, `bot_sealed_count>0`, `bot_serial_mirror_enabled`, and `!bot_serial_dead` at lines 2113-2117. The commit does not add per-entry state proving that the exact sealed page or entry was confirmed sent over serial.
- Impact: this creates a kernel code path that clears a sealed log record after write. Law 3 forbids clearing, truncating, or overwriting sealed log pages. Law 9 only permits reclaiming serial-confirmed sealed log pages; "serial mirror enabled and not dead" is weaker than a durable confirmation for the reclaimed record.
- Recommended follow-up: replace entry-level `MemSet` reclamation with page-level reclamation that is gated by explicit serial-confirmed state for that exact sealed page, or halt instead of clearing any unconfirmed sealed ledger memory.

### WARNING: Disk reclaim stage records success without deleting any lower-priority files

- Laws implicated: Law 9 Resource Supremacy / Crash on Log Failure, Law 5 North Star Discipline.
- Evidence: `BookTruthReclaimDisk(I64 need_pct,I64 pct)` only returns `1` when `need_pct>=95 || pct>=95`, with a comment that host-side disk cleanup is external, in `Kernel/BookOfTruth.HC` lines 2131-2138.
- Evidence: `BookTruthReclaimDo()` treats that `1` as `disk_count`, emits a reclaim payload, increments `bot_reclaim_disk`, and reports the disk stage at lines 2158-2178.
- Impact: the new disk stage can make status output claim a disk reclaim happened even though no non-log files, temp files, user files, or swap were actually deleted. This does not satisfy the Law 9 required order for disk pressure and can overstate CQ-085 progress.
- Recommended follow-up: either implement real local disk reclamation before counting disk-stage success, or report this as "disk reclaim required" with zero success and leave WS13-21/CQ disk deletion incomplete.

### WARNING: Reclaim exhaustion can continue without a fail-stop

- Laws implicated: Law 9 Resource Supremacy / Crash on Log Failure.
- Evidence: when pressure escalation fires, `BookTruthPressureTick()` calls `BookTruthReclaimDo(threshold,pct)` at lines 2883-2884 and then increments escalation counters at lines 2885-2888. There is no check for a zero return value and no `SysHlt` path when all reclaim stages fail.
- Impact: if the ledger remains above threshold and no task/cache/page/disk stage can reclaim resources, the kernel can continue running after the reclaim path reports no relief. Law 9 requires the OS to halt if the log cannot be protected after exhausting reclamation.
- Recommended follow-up: make reclaim exhaustion a terminal condition in the Book of Truth write/pressure path, with `SysHlt` in the same failure path after all eligible reclamation is exhausted.
