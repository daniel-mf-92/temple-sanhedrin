## Findings

### WARNING-1: MMIO evidence is encoded as generic DMA write records

Applicable laws:
- Law 8: Book of Truth Immediacy & Hardware Proximity
- Law 5: North Star Discipline

Evidence:
- `Kernel/IOMMU.HC:245-270` records off, deny, and allow outcomes through `BookTruthDMARecord(BOT_DMA_OP_WRITE, chan, 8, dev, ...)`.
- `Kernel/IOMMU.HC:247-259` compresses BAR and register into `chan=((bar&0xFF)<<8)|(reg&0xFF)`.
- `MODERNIZATION/MASTER_TASKS.md:269` marks WS14-11 done: GPU BAR/MMIO allowlist and unknown-write rejection.

Assessment:
The commit records an MMIO decision, but not as a first-class MMIO ledger event with full BAR, register width, value, and policy reason fields. Truncating BAR/register to 8-bit components and reusing `BOT_DMA_OP_WRITE` makes later Sanhedrin replay weaker: it cannot distinguish DMA writes from MMIO writes or reconstruct the exact register/value tuple.

Remediation:
- Add a canonical Book-of-Truth MMIO event or payload decoder before treating WS14-11 as fully evidenced.
- Preserve full BAR, register, width, value, allow/deny, and reason fields in the ledger payload.

### WARNING-2: Allowlist updates are not themselves Book-of-Truth events

Applicable laws:
- Law 3: Book of Truth Immutability
- Law 8: Book of Truth Immediacy & Hardware Proximity

Evidence:
- `Kernel/IOMMU.HC:203-232` adds/removes allowlist entries in `IOMMUGPUMMIOAllow`.
- That function mutates `iommu_gpu_mmio` but does not call a Book-of-Truth append/record routine.
- `Kernel/IOMMU.HC:235-274` logs only later write attempts.

Assessment:
The actual policy transition that makes a future MMIO write trusted is not synchronously recorded. If an allow rule appears or disappears without a ledger event, later MMIO allow/deny records lack an immutable explanation of who changed the rule, when, and under which profile. That is drift against the Law 8 hardware-proximity doctrine for security-sensitive control-plane changes.

Remediation:
- Emit a Book-of-Truth policy event from `IOMMUGPUMMIOAllow` for add, remove, duplicate, and capacity-denied outcomes.
- Include old/new rule counts and the full `(dev, bar, reg)` tuple.

### WARNING-3: Smoke coverage is grep-based and does not exercise MMIO policy behavior

Applicable laws:

Evidence:

- The script does not run a fixture that calls `IOMMUGPUMMIOInit`, denies an unknown write, allows a known rule, removes it, and checks counters/status output.
- `MODERNIZATION/MASTER_TASKS.md:269` marks WS14-11 done.

Assessment:
The smoke script is useful as a source-presence guard, and `bash -n` plus the script passed during this audit. It does not prove the deny-by-default semantics, counter behavior, or Book-of-Truth logging outcome described by the task. That is an evidence-quality warning rather than a direct law violation.

Remediation:
- Add a replay/static fixture with expected status lines for `rules_live`, `writes`, `allow`, and `deny`.
- Add negative assertions proving an unknown write is rejected and logged before any allowlist entry exists.
