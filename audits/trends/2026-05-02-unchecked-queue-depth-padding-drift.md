## Summary

The historical ledger has a queue-health accounting drift: many builder iterations passed while their own notes said queue depth was `unchecked`, and many inference rows explicitly mention appending, topping up, refilling, or maintaining queue items. That is material to Law 6 because queue depth is supposed to remain deep and traceable, and to the later no-self-generated-queue rule because builder-side queue padding should not be normalized as a passing iteration signal.

Findings: 5 total.

## Findings

### WARNING-1: Pass rows normalized unchecked queue depth as acceptable evidence

Evidence:
- `inference`: 384 `pass` rows contain `unchecked`.
- `modernization`: 184 `pass` rows contain `unchecked`.
- `sanhedrin`: 31 `pass` rows contain `unchecked`.
- Combined `pass` rows with either unchecked or queue-padding language: 479 inference rows, 195 modernization rows, and 31 Sanhedrin rows.

Impact: Law 6 requires queue health, but `unchecked` is the opposite of verified queue depth. The ledger should not let a row simultaneously claim pass status and leave queue depth unverified without a structured exception reason.

### WARNING-2: Inference notes repeatedly describe builder-side queue padding

Evidence:
- Inference rows containing `unchecked`: 384.
- Among those inference rows, 53 mention `appended`, 2 mention `topped up`, 8 mention `refill`, and 167 mention `maintained`.
- Explicit inference append/refill/top-up rows that also mention queue or task IDs: 192 rows from `2026-04-12T14:29:53` through `2026-04-23T08:28:20`.

Impact: The later no-self-generated-queue rule bans builder agents from adding new `CQ-` or `IQ-` lines to `MASTER_TASKS.md`. These historical notes show the behavior pattern existed before or around that rule becoming explicit, so older queue-depth compliance should be treated as suspect unless the queue additions can be tied to an external source.

### WARNING-3: Modernization also has unchecked queue-health rows, though less padding language

Evidence:
- Modernization rows containing `unchecked`: 184.
- Modernization rows with explicit append/refill/top-up language and queue or task IDs: 6 rows from `2026-04-12T15:38:11` through `2026-04-22T20:33:36`.
- Sample rows include notes such as queue depth remaining above threshold while unchecked.

Impact: The modernization loop had fewer explicit self-padding phrases than inference, but still often recorded queue depth as unverified. For Law 6 backfills, an unchecked queue-depth note should be classified as `unknown` or `warning`, not as positive evidence of queue health.

### WARNING-4: Queue-depth evidence is prose-only and cannot prove traceability

Evidence:
- `queue_depth_rows`: 248 inference rows, 191 modernization rows, and 14 Sanhedrin rows.
- `unchecked_rows`: 384 inference rows, 184 modernization rows, and 31 Sanhedrin rows.
- The database schema has no structured field for queue-depth count, source of new task IDs, monotonicity proof, duplicate check, or external trigger.

Impact: Retroactive Law 6 checks must scrape ambiguous notes to distinguish real queue health from self-padding. The current historical data can count claims, but it cannot prove that queue items trace back to a real WS task or were not generated by the builder.

### WARNING-5: Pass status hides whether queue work was verified, skipped, or only asserted

Evidence:
- Every `unchecked` row found in this query is `status='pass'`.
- Notes include formulations like `queue depth remains 21 unchecked IQ items`, `queue maintained at 15 unchecked`, and `queue depth remains >=25 unchecked`.

Impact: A pass row should mean the queue-health criterion was actually checked. If the queue was not checked, the row needs a separate structured field such as `queue_check_status='unchecked'` and should not be consumed as Law 6 evidence by later aggregation.

## Recommendations

- Treat historical `unchecked` queue-depth notes as Law 6 warning evidence, not pass evidence.
- Add structured fields for queue depth, duplicate check result, monotonic max ID, external trigger/source, and whether the builder appended task lines.
- Backfill no-self-generated-queue compliance by joining these suspect iteration rows to the corresponding `MASTER_TASKS.md` diffs in TempleOS and holyc-inference.
- Require builders to record queue health as `verified`, `unchecked`, or `not_applicable` separately from iteration `status`.
